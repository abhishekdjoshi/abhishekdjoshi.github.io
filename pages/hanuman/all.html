Who is Lord Hanuman?
Lord Hanuman is believed to be a god under Hinduism. He is believed to be a divine Vanara (a combination of 'Vana', meaning forest, and 'Nara', meaning animal, thus considered as forest-dwelling animal. In the modern world, Vanaras are depicted as monkeys). Lord Hanuman is also a supreme companion and devotee of Lord Rama, and both are central characters of Hindu epic Ramayana. In addition to being an ardent devotee of Lord Rama, Lord Hanuman is also believed to be one of the seven immortals per Hindu scriptures. It is also believed that Lord Hanuman is an avatar or incarnation of Lord Shiva, and is also believed to be son of the Lord Vayu, the god of wind.

According to scriptures, Lord Hanuman was born to Anjana, an angel who was cursed to earth, and father Kesari, a money king. Lord Hanuman is also called Vayu-Putra, the son of the Lord Vayu, the god of wind, because of Lord Vayu's role in Hanuman's birth. It is believed that when Anjana was worshiping, the King Dasharatha of Ayodhya was also performing the ritual in order to have children. As a result, he received some sacred pudding (payasam) to be shared by his three wives, leading to the births of Lord Rama, Lakshmana, Bharata and Shatrughna. By divine ordinance, a kite snatched a fragment of that pudding and dropped it while flying over the forest where Anjana was engaged in worship. The wind god, Lord Vayu, delivered the falling pudding to the outstretched hands of Anjana, who consumed it. Hanuman was born to her as a result.

Lord Hanuman is called Hanuman because of his disfigured jaw. In Sanskrit, 'Hanu' means jaw and 'Mana' means disfigured. According to scriptures, once up on a time in his childhood, Hanuman was hungry and saw the rising red colored sun. Mistaking it for a ripe fruit, he leapt up to eat it. Lord Indra, the king of gods, intervened and struck Hanuman with his thunderbolt. It hit Hanuman on his jaw, and he fell to the earth and got his jaw disfigured.

14 Attributes of Lord Hanuman
1. Immortal (also Chiranjeevi): Per the famous Hindu scripture Ramayana, it is believed that just before Lord Rama and Lakshmana die, Lord Hanuman is blessed to be immortal, and be a part of humanity forever.
2. Celibate and Self-Controlled (also Brahmachari): Lord Hanuman is one who controls lust from all materialistic things of this world.
3. Ugly on the Outside (Kurup) and Beautiful in the Inside (Sundar): In the scriptures, Lord Hanuman is described as ugly on the outside, but divinely beautiful in the inside. Lod Hanuman is believed to be the one with golden heart, full of devotion, love, compassion, strength and intelligence.
4. Shape Shifter (Kama Rupen): Lord Hanuman has the power to shapeshift, become smaller than the smallest, larger than the largest adversary at will. Lord Hamuman uses this power to shrink and enter Lanka, as he searches for the kidnapped Sita imprisoned by Ravana. Later on, he takes on the size of a mountain, blazing with radiance, to show his true power to Sita.
5. Strength: Lord Hanuman is extraordinarily strong, one capable of lifting and carrying any burden for a cause. He is called by many names such as Veer, Mahaveer and Mahabali Mahavira, Mahabala and others signifying his strength. During the epic war between Lord Rama and Ravana, Lord Hanuman leaps on mountains to find the particular herb to save the life of Lakshmana. On leaping the mountain, Lord Hanuman finds the mountain is full of many herbs and he doesn't know which herb to pick. So, Lord Hanuman lifts the entire Himalayan mountain and carries it across India to Lanka for Lakshmana. His immense strength thus helps Lakshmana recover from his wound.
6. Innovative: Lord Hanuman is described as someone who constantly faces tough situations and ambiguous challenges, where the adversary or circumstances threaten his mission with defeat and his very existence. Yet Lord Hanuman finds an innovative way to turn the odds. For example, when Lord Hanuman is arrested and tortured by Ravana's soldiers, and his tail is put on fire, Lord Hanuman leaps from one building to another building and burning the whole Lanka down in doing so.
7. Devotion (also Bhakti): Lord Hanuman is presented as the exemplary devotee of Lord Rama and Sita.
8. Learned Doyen: Lord Hanuman is believed to be an expert in Vedanta philosophy of Hinduism and the Vedas. Lord Hanuman is also believed to be a poet, a polymath, a grammarian, a singer and a musician par excellence.[47][1]
9. Remover of Obstacles: Lord Hanuman is believed to be the remover of all difficulties.
10. Bestower of Power (also Siddhis) and Treasures (also Nidhis): Lord Hanuman is described as the bestower of eight classical powers (the ability to become smaller than the smallest, reducing one's body to the size of an atom or even become invisible, the ability to become infinitely large, expanding one's body to an infinitely large size, the ability to become weightless or lighter than air, the ability to instantaneously travel or be anywhere at will, the ability to achieve or realize whatever one desires, the ability to control nature and organisms and establish supremacy over nature and ability to force influence upon anyone, the ability to control all material elements or natural forces, the ability to satisfy and suppress desire or make wishes come true) and nine treasures (lake double the size of padma in Himalaya with minerals and jewels, lake in Himalaya with minerals and jewels, conch shell, black antimony, tortoise or turtle shell, cinnabar or quicksilver, arsenic, antimony, cups or vessels baked in fire) - this was a boon granted to him by Sita.
11. Healer of Diseases, Pains and Sorrows: Lord Hanuman is believed to heal all kinds of diseases, pains and sorrows of devotees.
12. Slayer of Evil and Negative energies: Lord Hanuman is believed to keep away ghosts, evils, demons, devils, and all other kinds of negative energies. The following names of Hanuman describe some of these qualities: Raksho Vidhwan Sakaraka, Aksha Hantre, Dasha Greeva Kulantaka, Lanke Neebhanjana, Simhi Kaprana Bhanjana, Maha Ravana Mardana, Kalanemi Pramathana.
13. Protector of Devotees of Lord Rama and Himself: Lord Hanuman is believed to the doorkeeper and protector of the door to Lord Rama's court, and protector and saviour of devotees.
14. Five-Faced (also Panchmukhi): Lord Hanuman is believed to be Panchmukhi, a combination of 'Panch', meaning five, and 'Mukhi', meaning face, hence fived-faced. East facing face (Anjaneya) grants purity of mind and success. South facing face, which appears that of a man-lion (Karala Ugraveera Narasimha) grants victory and fearlessness. West facing face, which appears that of Eagle or Gardua (Mahaveera Garuda) grants protection from black magic and poisons. North facing face, which appears that of a boar, (Lakshmi Varaha) grants prosperity and wealth. Sky facing face, which appears that of a horse (Hayagriva) grants knowledge and good children.

10 Amazing Facts about Lord Hanuman
1. Lord Hanumand once applied sindoor all over his body for Lord Rama’s long life. Lord Hanuman was greatly devoted to Lord Rama. A particular incident was when Sita put sindoor on her forehead, Hanuman asked her why. To this, she replied that since she is the wife and companion of Lord Rama, the sindoor was a sign of her unconditional love and respect. Hanuman then covered his entire body with sindoor to prove his love for Lord Rama. Lord Rama was really impressed by this and granted a boon that those who worshiped Lord Hanuman in the future with sindoor would see all their difficulties go away.
2. Although he was a brahmchari, Lord Hanuman had a son - Makardhwaja. Lord Hanuman’s son Makardhwaja was born to a mighty fish of the same name when Lord Hanuman after burning the entire Lanka with his tail had dipped in the sea to cool off his body. It is said that Lord Hanuman's sweat was swallowed by the fish and thus Makardhwaja was conceived.
3. Once, Lord Rama issued a death sentence to Lord Hanuman. After Lord Rama became the king, once, when the court was adjourned, Narada – known for creating disharmony between Lord Rama and Lord Hanuman – asked Lord Hanuman to greet all the sages except Sage Vishwamitra, since he was a king once. Lord Hanuman did so, but it did not affect Sage Vishwamitra. Narada went on and instigated Sage Vishwamitra, which angered him so much that he went to Lord Rama and asked him to punish Hanuman to death. Sage Vishmamitra being his guru, Lord Rama could not ignore his command and punished Lord Hanuman to death by arrows. Next day in the field, the statement was to be executed, but all arrows failed to do any harm to Lord Hanuman as he kept chanting Lord Rama's name. Since Lord Ram had to abide by his guru’s word, he decided to use the Brahmastra. To the surprise of all, Hanuman’s chants of Ram even failed the most powerful Brahmastra. Seeing this, Narada went to Sage Vishwamitra and confessed his mistake, stopping the ordeal.
4. Lord Hanuman and Bhima were both brothers. Bhima was also the son of Vayu (the Lord of the Winds). One day, when Bhima was searching for a flower his wife wanted, he saw a monkey sleeping with his tail crossing the path. He asked him to move his tail. But the monkey didn’t do it and asked Bhima to move it. Bhima was very arrogant with his strength. Nevertheless, he couldn’t move or lift the tail. Therefore, he realized that this was not a mere ordinary monkey. It was none other than Lord Hanuman. He lied there just to reduce the arrogance of Bhima.
5. When Lord Rama’s time of death was at hand, Lord Hanuman barred Yama from claiming him. When Lord Rama decided to leave his earthly existence for his journey to Vaikuntha (heavenly abode of Lord Vishnu), he knew that Lord Hanuman would not let him do so as he was a great devotee. So he instructed Lord Hanuman to find his ring which had fallen on the ground and then disappeared in the Patal Loka. Lord Hanuman went on the task of finding the ring and was met by the Yama. He told him that the falling of the ring signified that the time for the end of Lord Ram's avatar had come.
6. Lord Hanuman once rejected Goddess Sita's gift. When Goddess Sita gave Lord Hanuman a beautiful pearl necklace as a gift he politely declined it saying he doesn’t accept anything that is devoid of Lord Rama’s name. To prove his point, the ardent devotee then ripped off his chest to reveal an image of both of them.
7. Lord Hanuman has 108 names.
8. Hanuman’s hunger saga. Once Lord Hanuman visited Goddess Sita in Sage Valmiki’s cottage and expressed his desire to eat some food cooked by Goddess Sita, who started cooking many dishes and started serving Lord Hanuman. But Lord Hanuman’s hunger was unquenchable and the entire rations of the house were coming to an end and finally Goddess Sita had to pray Lord Rama. Then Lord Hanuman suggested Goddess Sita serve a morsel with a tulsi leaf and then his hunger was finally satisfied.
9. Lord Hanuman was kind of a naughty kid in his childhood and he often used to tease the meditating sages in the forests. Finding Lord Hanuman’s unbearable acts, but realizing that he was but a child, the sages placed a mild curse on him by which he became unable to remember his own ability unless reminded by another person. The curse of the sages is featured in Kishkindha Kanda and Sundara Kanda when Jambavantha reminds Lord Hanuman of his abilities and encourages him to go and find Goddess Sita.
10. After the birth of Lord Hanuman, Lord Varun blessed Lord Hanuman with a boon that he would always be protected from water. Lord Agni blessed him saying, “Fire will never burn you.” Lord Surya blessed him with two siddhis of yoga to help Lord Hanuman attain the smallest form and the biggest form at will. Lord Vayu showered Lord Hanuman with more speed than the speed of himself, the wind. Lord Yama (the God of Death) blessed him with a healthy life.

Why do followers believe in Lord Hanuman?
Lord Hanuman is symbol of strength and energy. Lord Hanuman is worshipped for his unyielding devotion to Lord Rama and is remembered for his selfless dedication to the Lord Rama. Lord Hanuman is considered the living embodiment of the Karma Yogi (one whose meditation and devotion are demonstrated through hard work or service). In return for his unconditional love, Lord Rama granted him everlasting life. He promised that he would be worshipped alongside Lord Rama and that his idol would be placed next to his.

Followers also believe in Lord Hanuman for his ideal qualities:
1. Lord Hanuman was a scholar par excellence.He knew all the nine vyakaranas.(grammatical works in Sanskrit)
2. Lord Hanuman had immense strength.
3. Lord Hanuman had immense popularity among Vaanaras and all his opponents were fearful of him due to his fame.
4. Lord Hanuman was extremely courageous. His courage took him to Lanka after his travel across the ocean and his fearlessness startled Ravan himself.
5. Lord Hanuman was extremely fit and agile at the same time. Laziness was never his quality due to his ever ready nature.
6. Lord Hanuman was eloquent and purposeful. His speech soothed Goddess Sita in captivity. His speech defied Ravana and warned Ravana of the impending consequences of his behaviour towards Lord Rama.

What are the benefits of worshipping Lord Hanuman?
It is believed that by worshipping Lord Hanuman, one is provided with strength, both physical and mental and knowledge and all the pains, troubles, imperfections and impurities are removed. Lord Hanuman also protects from evil effects and evil spirits.

It is believed that worshipping Hanuman helps one stay disciplined, achieve greater heights in life and career. Prayers to Lord Hanuman also help one overcome bad habits like cheating, adultery, laziness, fickle mindedness, procrastination etc. It brings in confidence and stable mind. It is also believed that if one is affected by Shani dosha then praying to Lord Hanuman is beneficial.

Which day of the week is Lord Hanuman worhsipped?
Most people pray to Lord Hanuman either on Tuesdays or Saturdays either by visiting Hanuman temples or at home and can also chant Hanuman Chalisa every day to get his blessings.

Devotees worship Lord Hanuman on Saturdays to ward off the ill-effects of Lord Shani. And there's a story associated with this belief. Ravana, the demon-king of Lanka, had become so powerful that he could control the movements of the planets as per his will. During the time of his son Meghnad's birth, Ravana wanted his natal chart to be so dominant that he could remain invincible all his life. However, Lord Shani, who plays a vital role in shaping people's lives, played a trick when Ravana moved him on the birth chart. Hence, to punish Lord Shani, Ravana held him captive in a dark prison and hung him upside down. Subsequently, when Lord Hanuman visited Lanka in search of Goddess Sita, Lord Hanuman heard the faintly voice of Lord Shani, crying for help. Lord Hanuman stepped forward to help and rescued Lord Shani from the clutches of Ravana. Therefore, to thank Lord Hanuman for his humble gesture and return the favour, Lord Shani promised that he would never cast his effects on him. He also said that those who would worship Lord Hanuman on Saturdays would be relieved of his adverse effects.

Which festivals in celebration of Lord Hanuman?
Hanuman Jayanti is celebrated to commemorate the birth of Lord Hanuman who is also considered to be one of the incarnations of Lord Shiva. On this day, Hanuman Chalisa - a set of prayers glorifying Lord Hanuman - is recited. Depending on the devotee, the text is either recited non-stop for 24 hours or sometimes it's performed a set number of times. Special Pujas are performed and offerings are made to Lord Hanuman. Some people have different rituals, such as sacred fire ceremonies. In some places, especially in India, colourful processions fill the streets. People dance, carry idols of Lord Hanuman and some people wear masks and tails to imitate Lord Hanuman. Every celebration is always accompanied by a period of fasting and then a big feast.
